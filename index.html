Australia Weather Temperature Analysis

Author: Julien Siew Wilson

The data analysed is sourced from the Australian Bureau of Meteorology (BoM) and the Australian Climate Service (ACS)

Top stations by annual mean temperature are more likely found in Australia’s north and interior; each station's values are tightly packed, suggesting small differences at higher temperatures.
Locations with the highest average maximums are largely NT/WA outposts (e.g., Katherine/Darwin region), consistent with tropical and semi-arid climates. Major Cities do show a better contrast of temperature, suggesting a locational affect on the temperature across regions in Australia.
With Sunshine as variable to temperature, this plot shows a slight positive relationship where more sunshine generally accompanies higher max temperatures up to ~35 °C; variability increases in mid-ranges across cities.
With Rainfall as a variable, this plot shows no clear relation to temperature heavier rain events appear at moderate temperatures instead; in fact, the hottest days tend to have lower rainfall, hinting at drier hot conditions.
With tihs geological map, it is more likely seen that warmer stations concentrate in the Top End and inland areas; whereby, cooler means appear in the south and higher-latitude coastal regions.
